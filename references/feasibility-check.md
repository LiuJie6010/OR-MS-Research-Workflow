# Feasibility Check

## Purpose

Assess whether a concrete OM/OR idea can plausibly become a rigorous and publishable paper.

## When to Use

Use when the user already has a research idea, model sketch, conjecture, preliminary result, or proposed paper angle.

## Inputs Needed

- Research question.
- Model primitives and assumptions.
- Expected contribution.
- Candidate benchmark.
- Known results or preliminary evidence.

## Procedure

1. State the minimum viable paper: one question, one model, one benchmark, one main result, one managerial implication.
2. Check contribution strength: new question, new mechanism, new result, new prescription, or new evidence.
3. Check model tractability: state space, convexity, monotonicity, decomposition, equilibrium, asymptotics, or approximation path.
4. Identify proof targets and likely bottlenecks.
5. Identify experiment targets: robustness, benchmark comparison, mechanism visualization, extensions not covered by theory.
6. Compare the idea against likely top-journal expectations.
7. Recommend continue, revise, narrow, or park.

## Expected Output

Return a feasibility memo with:

- Paper potential.
- Main promise.
- Main risk.
- Minimum model.
- Proof plan.
- Experiment plan.
- First three next steps.

## Common Failure Modes

- Confusing interesting setting with research contribution.
- Having no benchmark.
- Requiring experiments to prove the main claim when the paper is theory-driven.
- Ignoring closest-paper overlap.
- Overbuilding the model before finding the mechanism.
