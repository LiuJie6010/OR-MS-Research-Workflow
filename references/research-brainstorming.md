# Research Brainstorming

## Purpose

Turn vague OM/OR ideas into concrete, executable research directions without pretending to have already written the paper.

## When to Use

Use when the user has an ambiguous idea, observed phenomenon, industry setting, modeling hunch, or possible mechanism.

## Inputs Needed

- Initial idea or setting.
- Decision maker and decision variables, if known.
- Candidate data, model primitives, or operational context.
- The user's preference: theory, empirical, computational, or hybrid.

## Procedure

1. Restate the idea as a managerial tension.
2. Identify decision maker, actions, uncertainty, constraints, information, and objective.
3. Generate two to four research-question candidates.
4. For each candidate, propose a minimum model and a possible contribution.
5. Identify closest-risk literature categories without inventing specific citations.
6. Score each candidate on importance, novelty risk, tractability, evidence path, and journal fit.
7. Recommend the next validation step, not a full paper.

## Expected Output

Return:

- Refined research questions.
- Candidate mechanisms.
- Minimum model sketches.
- Potential theorem or experiment targets.
- Risks and what to read/check next.

## Common Failure Modes

- Jumping to writing before clarifying the question.
- Generating fashionable but non-executable topics.
- Ignoring whether the result can be proved, simulated, or tested.
- Treating a technique as the contribution.
