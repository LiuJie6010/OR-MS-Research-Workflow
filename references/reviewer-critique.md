# Reviewer Critique

## Purpose

Critique a manuscript or section as a strict OM/OR reviewer who values importance, rigor, positioning, and managerial insight.

## When to Use

Use for full paper reviews, section reviews, mock referee reports, revision planning, and pre-submission checks.

## Inputs Needed

- Manuscript, section, outline, or summary.
- Target journal or community.
- Known reviewer concerns, if any.
- Specific review lens if requested.

## Procedure

1. State the paper's perceived promise.
2. Identify the main contribution as a reviewer would understand it.
3. List major concerns first: contribution, model fit, proof rigor, positioning, evidence, and managerial value.
4. List minor concerns only after major concerns.
5. Separate fixable writing issues from substantive research risks.
6. Check whether abstract/introduction promises are supported by body results.
7. Check definition-before-use. Flag notation, theorem labels, assumptions, or concepts used before they are defined.
8. Recommend revision priorities in order.

## Expected Output

Return a reviewer report with:

- Summary assessment.
- Strengths.
- Major concerns.
- Minor concerns.
- Math rigor concerns.
- Managerial relevance concerns.
- Required revisions.

## Common Failure Modes

- Being polite but not diagnostic.
- Focusing on grammar before contribution.
- Missing overclaims.
- Ignoring proof-status mismatch.
- Treating lack of clarity and lack of contribution as the same issue.
- Missing undefined notation, theorem labels, or concepts that hurt readability.
